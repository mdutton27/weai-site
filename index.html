Data integrity · the practical part

Garbage in,
garbage at scale.

AI doesn't just inherit your data quality — it amplifies it. A messy spreadsheet becomes a confidently-wrong assistant. Here's how we keep that from happening.

/01

Source of truth, named.

Before we connect anything to a model, we agree which system is authoritative for which fact. The CRM owns the customer; Xero owns the invoice. AI reads from the source — never from a copy of a copy.

/02

Provenance on every output.

Every AI-drafted answer carries its sources. If the model says “your Q3 revenue was $1.4M,” you can click through to the rows it pulled. No source, no answer.

/03

Human in the send loop.

For anything that leaves your business — a quote, an email, an invoice — a person reviews and signs off. The AI prepares; the human ships. We design the workflow that way on purpose.

/04

Drift detection, built in.

Models go stale. Data shifts. We instrument every assistant with quality checks — confidence scores, escalation rules, weekly drift reports — so you find out from us, not from a customer.

/05

Hosted in NZ jurisdiction.

Your data stays under NZ law. We default to providers with Australia/NZ regions and explicit no-training contracts — your business doesn’t become someone else’s training data.

/06

An off-switch that works.

Every system we build can be paused or rolled back in a single command. If it goes sideways at 4pm on a Friday, we can stop it before close of business — not next Tuesday after a vendor ticket.
